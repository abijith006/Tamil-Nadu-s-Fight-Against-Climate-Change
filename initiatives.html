Current Status

The Tamil Nadu Climate Change Mission is a pioneering initiative aimed at achieving net-zero emissions by 2070. The mission focuses on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, enhancing biodiversity, and promoting sustainable development. Through the Green Tamil Nadu Mission, over 8.3 crore saplings have been planted across the state, significantly increasing green cover and carbon sequestration.

Key objectives of the mission include:

  • Promoting renewable energy sources like solar and wind power.
  • Implementing energy-efficient practices in industries and households.
  • Restoring degraded ecosystems and protecting biodiversity.

For more details, visit the Tamil Nadu Climate Change Mission website.

How You Can Help

Every individual can contribute to the fight against climate change. Here are some ways you can help:

  • Participate in tree planting drives organized by the Green Tamil Nadu Mission.
  • Reduce the use of single-use plastics and switch to eco-friendly alternatives.
  • Support the "Meendum Manjappai" campaign by using cloth bags instead of plastic bags.
  • Adopt sustainable practices like rainwater harvesting and waste segregation.

Join the Meendum Manjappai initiative to make a difference.

Activities Study

The Tamil Nadu Climate Change Mission has outlined several key activities for the years 2021-2023. These activities are designed to address various aspects of climate change, including mitigation, adaptation, and sustainable development. Below is a summary of the proposed activities:

2021-2022 Activities

  • Reducing Emissions from Mass Transport: Baseline study to reduce emissions from public transport systems.
  • Rehabilitation of Coastal Habitats: Formation of bio-shields using eco-friendly solutions like mangroves and seagrass beds.
  • Waste to Energy: Baseline study for clean power generation from waste.
  • Carbon Enrichment Programme: Soil carbon storage to promote tree growth and forest expansion.
  • Sustainable Habitat: Energy-saving measures in buildings to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Climate Literacy: Awareness campaigns and media plans to educate the public on climate change.
  • Climate Smart Villages: Pilot projects to test climate-resilient agricultural practices.

2022-2023 Activities

  • Stakeholder Workshops: Capacity-building workshops for local communities and government departments.
  • Rehabilitation of Coastal Habitats: Continued efforts to protect coastal areas through bio-shields and mangrove plantations.
  • Carbon Enrichment Programme: Pilot project to enhance soil carbon storage in degraded forests.
  • Sustainable Habitat: Energy-saving measures in government and private buildings.
  • Climate Literacy: Awareness campaigns targeting students, farmers, and industries.
  • Climate Smart Villages: Expansion of pilot projects to more villages.
  • Climate Resilient Green Temples: Pilot projects to make temples climate-resilient through solar lighting and water management.

For more details on these activities, visit the 2021-2022 Projects and 2022-2023 Projects pages.

Climate Summit 1.0

Tamil Nadu is the first state in India to organize a Climate Summit to bring in national and international best practices to address the climate and biodiversity crises. The summit was conducted on 8th and 9th December 2022 in Chennai, with participation from national and international experts, partner organizations, reputed institutions, and stakeholders.
